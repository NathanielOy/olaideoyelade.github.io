Introduction

Welcome! I am a researcher at North Carolina A&T University specializing in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Biomedical Informatics.

My academic and research efforts focus on leveraging advanced generative and deep learning frameworks to solve critical challenges in healthcare and structural biology. A major pillar of my current work centers on AI-driven drug discovery, where I design computational models for targeted molecular generation and analyze complex protein-protein interactions (PPI) to identify potential therapeutic candidates and accelerate modern medicine.

Alongside molecular biology applications, I work extensively on algorithm optimization using bio-inspired metaheuristic search techniques and robust clustering frameworks. By engineering intelligent neural architectures and automated data-driven systems, my research bridges the gap between deep learning and clinical application—addressing vital medical domains from oncology image analysis to digital screening systems.

Key Research Highlights

  • 🎗️ Oncology Image Analysis: Designed twin convolutional networks and generative adversarial networks (GANs) dedicated to breast cancer detection and synthetic region-of-interest modeling.
  • 💊 Drug Discovery: Leveraging molecular generation techniques to discover novel therapeutics, with active drafts under review and published work on generative frameworks for targeted drug design.
  • 🦟 Metaheuristic Architectures: Developed and improved nature-inspired optimization models (such as Ebola optimization and dwarf mongoose optimization models) for feature engineering and complex design tasks.
  • 📈 Clustering Frameworks: Contributed state-of-the-art comprehensive surveys and taxonomies detailing machine learning clustering methodologies.
