Biography

Welcome! I am a researcher at North Carolina A&T University specializing in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Biomedical Informatics.

My academic and research efforts focus on leveraging intelligent computational frameworks to solve complex, high-dimensional data problems. I design, evaluate, and optimize machine learning and deep learning models to extract actionable insights from medical data, contributing significantly to modern healthcare applications and computer vision frameworks.

A major track of my work involves algorithm optimization and bio-inspired metaheuristic search techniques. By engineering robust, automatic clustering algorithms and advanced neural architectures, my research helps address critical domains like breast cancer detection, digital mammography analysis, and automated disease screening systems.

Key Research Highlights

  • 📈 Clustering Frameworks: Contributed state-of-the-art comprehensive surveys and taxonomies detailing machine learning clustering methodologies.
  • 🦟 Metaheuristic Architectures: Developed and improved nature-inspired optimization models (such as Ebola optimization and dwarf mongoose optimization models) for feature engineering and complex design tasks.
  • 🎗️ Oncology Image Analysis: Designed twin convolutional networks and generative adversarial networks (GANs) dedicated to breast cancer detection and synthetic region-of-interest modeling.
